I love to learn.

As a software engineer my profession is constantly changing and evolving and so I feel a key aspect at being the best I can be is to keep up to date with latest programming techniques, softwares, methodologies, ect. I utilize many free resources such as khan academy, codeacademy, freecodecamp.com, lynda.com, w3schools.com, ect. and I've donated to many of these to help support their cause.

I love to teach.

In late 2007 and early 2008 I worked as a math and physics tutor at the Bluegrass Community and Technical College in Lexington, KY. To know something is one thing but to be able to explain it in a way that makes sense to someone that is struggling with those concepts is an artform in itself. I truly admire teachers and professors for this unique skillset. This experience is what drove me to my senior thesis while pursuing a Bachelors of Science in Computer Science and Mathematics. I created a web application that solved math, physics, statics and engineering related problems step by step to help users learn and its something I'd like to improve on in the near future.

I love to be creative.

I do that in many different areas. Programming and ui development definitely allows me to express myself virtually but I also enjoy woodworking and art. I have a facebook woodworking page that I've maintained off and on since 2010 and a programming facebook page I started around 2011.

I love my country.

In August 2004 I enlisted with the United States Army reserve as a 21T (Technical Engineer). After nine years, two deployments and several ATs I was honorably discharged. I have gotten to see and do things that most could not imagine. It has allowed me to be truly grateful for what we have in America as well as giving me the ability to be truly cognizant of some of the struggles many others around the world face every day. I have had the privilege to Survey and Design everything from schools and soccer fields in El Salvador to helicopter landing pads in Iraq.

I love what I do.

I currently work as a software/web developer for Toyota for their Powertrain Engineering Department in Georgetown, Kentucky.
